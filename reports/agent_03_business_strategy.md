# Agent 3: Business Strategy Report
## Victoria Royals -- Concession Revenue Optimization Strategy
### Save-On-Foods Memorial Centre (SOFMC), Victoria, BC

---

## Executive Summary

The Victoria Royals concession operation at SOFMC represents a solid foundation with significant untapped upside. Based on analysis of ~239,000 transactions across ~62 games over two seasons, the operation generates an estimated **$1.65M-$1.85M in annual gross concession revenue** across 6 primary stands plus portable stations. The current estimated per-cap spend of **$10.20-$11.50** is approximately **25-35% below the industry benchmark** of $14-$16 for comparable minor league/junior hockey venues in Canada.

This report identifies a realistic path to **$350,000-$550,000 in incremental annual revenue** (a 20-30% lift) through a phased strategy prioritizing low-cost, high-impact initiatives. The three highest-impact opportunities are:

1. **Solving the Intermission Bottleneck** -- an estimated $80,000-$120,000 in currently lost revenue per season due to line abandonment during 18-minute intermissions.
2. **Combo/Bundle Strategy ("Power Play Combos")** -- driving average basket size up by $2.50-$4.00 per transaction, worth $95,000-$150,000 annually.
3. **Attendance-Based Dynamic Stand Operations** -- reducing labor and waste costs by $40,000-$60,000 while improving service on high-attendance nights.

Every recommendation is designed to be self-funding within the first season, reflecting the reality of WHL-level budgets and thin concession margins.

## 3. The Intermission Revenue Problem & Solutions

### 3.1 Quantifying the Problem

The intermission window is the single largest revenue constraint in arena concessions:

| Parameter | Value | Source |
|---|---|---|
| Intermission length | 18 minutes | Standard WHL |
| Usable service time | ~15 minutes | Allowing for travel time |
| Peak demand window | First 8-10 minutes | Industry standard |
| Avg transactions per minute (per stand) | 3-5 | Industry benchmark |
| Stands open per intermission | 4-6 | Varies by attendance |
| Max throughput per intermission | 1,080-1,800 transactions | 4-6 stands x 4 tx/min x 15 min |
| Estimated demand per intermission | 1,500-2,500 transactions | 40-60% of attendees |
| **Estimated lost transactions per intermission** | **200-700** | **Demand minus capacity** |
| **Lost transactions per game (2 intermissions)** | **400-1,400** | |
| **Lost revenue per game** | **$2,800-$11,200** | At ~$8 avg transaction |
| **Lost revenue per season (31 games)** | **$86,800-$347,200** | |

**Conservative estimate of annual lost intermission revenue: $80,000-$120,000**

### 3.2 Solution Framework: The "3-Lane" Intermission Strategy

#### Lane 1: Express Grab-and-Go (Cost: $2,000-$5,000)

**Concept**: Pre-made items in clear-lid containers staged 10 minutes before each intermission. No cooking, no customization -- just grab and tap.

| Element | Detail |
|---|---|
| What to sell | Pre-poured draft beer (in sealed cups), bottled beer, hot dogs (pre-wrapped), popcorn bags, water, pop, candy |
| Where | Convert one portable station to permanent "Express Lane" on high-traffic concourse |
| Staffing | 2 staff: 1 runner/stocker, 1 cashier |
| Speed target | 15-20 seconds per transaction (vs 45-90 seconds at full stands) |
| Throughput gain | +180-300 additional transactions per intermission |

**ROI**: $2,500 setup (signage, display cart, supplies) generates an estimated $50,000-$80,000 in additional annual revenue. **ROI: 1,000-3,200%.**

#### Lane 2: Expand Mobile Ordering Beyond ReMax Fan Deck (Cost: $5,000-$15,000)

**Concept**: Extend the existing Eventium QR-code system (currently ReMax Fan Deck only) to 2-3 additional sections.

| Element | Detail |
|---|---|
| Technology | Eventium already deployed -- negotiate section expansion |
| Target sections | Two highest-traffic general seating sections nearest Island Canteen |
| Fulfillment | Dedicated pickup window or runner delivery |
| Incentive | "Skip the line" messaging -- no discount needed, convenience is the value |
| Cannibalization risk | Low -- these fans were going to buy anyway; mobile ordering captures the ones who would have given up |

**ROI**: $10,000 implementation + Eventium fees. Estimated $40,000-$60,000 in recovered revenue. **ROI: 300-500%.**

#### Lane 3: Pre-Order Before Intermission (Cost: $1,000-$3,000)

**Concept**: Simple QR code on seats/screens allowing fans to order during play and pick up at intermission start.

| Element | Detail |
|---|---|
| Technology | Google Form or simple web form linked to stand POS (Phase 1 low-tech); Eventium expansion (Phase 2) |
| Display | QR code on concourse TV screens, seat-back stickers, in-arena PA announcement |
| Pickup | Numbered orders at dedicated pickup counter |
| Timing | Orders placed during 2nd/3rd period, ready at intermission start |

**ROI**: $2,000 setup. Estimated $15,000-$25,000 in additional captured revenue. **ROI: 500-1,250%.**

### 3.3 Concourse Flow Optimization (Cost: $500-$2,000)

- Place Island Canteen overflow signage directing to Island Slice and TacoTacoTaco during peak times
- Use floor decals or rope lines to manage queue flow
- Digital counter displays showing estimated wait times at each stand (low-tech: staff member with a whiteboard; high-tech: camera-based counting)

### 4.3 Attendance-Based Stand Opening Strategy

Currently all stands likely open for all games, which is inefficient for low-attendance weekday games (1,200-2,200).

| Attendance Tier | Expected Attendance | Stands to Open | Staffing |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Low** (Tue/Wed, non-rival) | 1,200-1,800 | Island Canteen, Phillips Bar, 1 Portable | 8-12 staff |

**Savings**: Reducing staffing by 8-12 positions on ~10 low-attendance games saves an estimated **$15,000-$25,000/season** in labor costs, while reducing food waste from overstocking low-volume stands.

### 4.4 Menu Simplification

The operation currently offers approximately 41 distinct items. For a 7,000-seat WHL arena, this is on the high end. Each additional item adds complexity to ordering, training, inventory, and prep.

**Recommendation**: Reduce to 30-32 core items by:

| Action | Items Affected | Impact |
|---|---|---|
| Eliminate lowest-selling 5 items | Review bottom 5 by volume | Simplify inventory, reduce waste |
| Consolidate candy/sweet SKUs | Keep top 3 candy bars, top 2 gummy options | Reduce supplier complexity |
| Standardize beverage options | Reduce pop flavors from ~8 to 5-6 | Faster ordering, less waste |

**Cost savings**: $5,000-$10,000/season in reduced waste and procurement complexity.

## 7. Cost Optimization Opportunities

### 7.1 Inventory Management & Waste Reduction

**Current state**: Without a predictive model, stands likely over-prep for high-attendance and under-prep for low-attendance, leading to waste and stockouts.

#### Attendance-Based Prep Model

Using the historical attendance data (GameDetails.xlsx), build a simple prep matrix:

| Factor | Data Available | Predictive Value |
|---|---|---|
| Day of week | Yes | High -- Fridays 2x weekdays |
| Opponent | Yes | High -- Vancouver draws 2-3x |
| Season timing | Yes | Medium -- early season lower |
| Playoffs | Yes | Very high -- 3,300-5,500 |
| Weather/holiday | Partially | Medium |

**Simple Prep Formula** (implementable in a spreadsheet):

```
Base prep = (Predicted attendance x Per-cap items) x Category share%
Safety stock = Base prep x 15% (for perishable) or x 25% (for non-perishable)
```

| Attendance Prediction | Hot Dogs | Beer (cans) | Pizza | Popcorn |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1,500 (low weekday) | 180 | 300 | 80 | 120 |
| 2,600 (average) | 310 | 520 | 140 | 210 |
| 4,000 (good Friday) | 480 | 800 | 215 | 320 |
| 5,500 (playoff) | 660 | 1,100 | 295 | 440 |

**Cost**: $0 (spreadsheet-based, no technology investment)
**Savings**: $15,000-$25,000/season in reduced food waste (estimated 8-12% of perishable COGS currently wasted)

## Risk Assessment & Mitigation

### Risk 1: Price Increases Drive Down Volume
- **Likelihood**: Low-Medium (craft beer drinkers are less price sensitive)
- **Impact**: Moderate (could offset gains)
- **Mitigation**: Implement increases gradually. Start with $0.50 on craft beer only. Monitor per-game volume for 4-6 games. Roll back if volume drops >5%.
- **Monitoring**: Track units sold per category per game vs. prior season baseline.

### Risk 2: Combo Cannibalization
- **Likelihood**: Medium (some customers who would have bought individually now get a discount)
- **Impact**: Low-Moderate (combos have lower per-item margin but higher total transaction value)
- **Mitigation**: Price combos at no more than 15-20% discount. Focus combos on driving a second or third item purchase, not discounting single items. Monitor gross margin per transaction, not just revenue.

### Risk 3: Mobile Ordering Cannibalizes Stand Sales
- **Likelihood**: Low (the client raised this concern, but evidence from comparable venues shows mobile ordering captures otherwise-lost sales, not redirects from existing sales)
- **Impact**: Low (net positive even with some cannibalization)
- **Mitigation**: Track total per-game revenue before and after mobile ordering expansion. If stand revenue drops, the mobile orders should more than compensate.

### Risk 4: Staffing Reduction Leads to Poor Service
- **Likelihood**: Medium (reducing staff on low-attendance nights requires accurate attendance prediction)
- **Impact**: High (poor service creates negative word-of-mouth)
- **Mitigation**: Set minimum staffing floors per stand (never below 2 per open stand). Use the prediction model as a guide, not a mandate. Keep 2-3 "on-call" staff for unexpected surges.

### Risk 5: Eventium/Technology Partner Costs Escalate
- **Likelihood**: Medium (SaaS vendors often raise prices after lock-in)
- **Impact**: Moderate (could erode mobile ordering ROI)
- **Mitigation**: Negotiate multi-year pricing with Eventium before expanding. Include performance clauses tied to transaction volume. Maintain ability to switch to alternative providers (e.g., FanFood, Bypass, Appetize).

### Risk 6: Food Safety with Express/Grab-and-Go
- **Likelihood**: Low (if proper protocols followed)
- **Impact**: Very High (food safety incident would be catastrophic for brand and operations)
- **Mitigation**: All grab-and-go items must follow BC food safety holding temperature and time requirements. Hot items in warming trays, cold items on ice. Discard all prepared items after 2-hour window. Train express staff on food safety specifically.

### Risk 7: Low Adoption of Loyalty/Pre-Order Programs
- **Likelihood**: Medium-High (WHL audience skews younger/family; may not engage with programs)
- **Impact**: Low (programs are low-cost, so break-even threshold is very low)
- **Mitigation**: Keep programs dead simple -- physical stamp cards require zero technology from the fan. Promote heavily via jumbotron, PA, and social media. Set a 3-game trial; if engagement is under 5%, pivot the approach before investing more.

### Risk 8: Sponsorship Sales Fall Short
- **Likelihood**: Medium (Victoria is a smaller market; limited pool of sponsors)
- **Impact**: Low-Moderate (sponsorship is upside, not foundational to the strategy)
- **Mitigation**: Start with existing partners (Phillips, Strait & Narrow, Cinco) who already have brand presence and are invested. Offer pilot packages at lower cost to prove ROI before asking for full-price commitments.
