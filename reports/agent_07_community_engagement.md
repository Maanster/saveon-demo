# Agent 7: Market Impact & Community Engagement Report
## Victoria Royals -- Community Hub & Visiting Fan Strategy

---

### Executive Summary

The Victoria Royals occupy a unique strategic position: a WHL franchise in a provincial capital with a captive island market, a passionate craft beverage culture, and a downtown arena within walking distance of hotels, restaurants, and tourism infrastructure. However, with average attendance around 2,600 in a 7,000-seat venue, and weekday games regularly dipping below 1,500, the team is leaving significant concession revenue on empty seats.

This report identifies three high-impact revenue levers for concession growth through community engagement:

1. **Visiting Fan Capture** -- Vancouver Giants games already draw 2,200-4,900 fans. A structured "Royals Road Trip" package with BC Ferries and downtown hospitality partners can increase visiting fan attendance by 15-25% on targeted games, directly boosting concession per-caps since visiting fans over-index on food/beverage spending (they have no kitchen to go home to).

2. **Weekday Game Activation** -- Tuesday/Wednesday games averaging 1,200-2,200 represent the single largest revenue gap. Community group nights, corporate packages, and themed promotions (the $1 Dog Night proof-of-concept drew 2,245 on a Wednesday) can lift weekday attendance by 20-40%, translating to $3,000-$8,000 in incremental concession revenue per game.

3. **Local Business Partnership Network** -- Victoria's dense downtown business ecosystem enables a "Royals District" model where 15-25 partner businesses cross-promote game nights, driving new fans to the arena while giving partners game-night foot traffic. This is largely partner-funded and generates sponsorship revenue.

**Total Estimated Annual Concession Revenue Impact: $150,000 - $350,000** across all strategies, with most initiatives requiring less than $5,000 in upfront investment and funded primarily through partnerships.

### 1. Visiting Fan Capture Strategy (by Opponent)

#### 1.1 The Opportunity

Visiting fans represent the single most under-monetized segment in the SOFMC concession ecosystem. Key data points:

- Vancouver Giants games draw 2,247 to 4,942 -- a 73% to 290% premium over average Tuesday/Wednesday games
- Prairie team visits (Red Deer: 4,217; Calgary: 2,989; Edmonton: 2,896) show strong "novelty draw" potential
- Playoff Spokane drew the all-time high of 5,540
- Visiting fans traveling by ferry are committed to a full evening -- they cannot leave early, increasing dwell time and concession windows

**Revenue implication:** If a Vancouver game draws 4,000 vs. a typical Wednesday's 1,500, that is 2,500 incremental fans. At a conservative $8-$12 per-cap on concessions, that is $20,000-$30,000 in additional concession revenue per Vancouver game. With 2-4 Vancouver visits per season, this segment alone represents $40,000-$120,000 in annual concession upside -- much of it already being captured, but with room to grow.

#### 1.2 Strategy by Opponent Tier

**Tier 1: Vancouver Giants (Highest Priority)**

The Giants are the Royals' concession revenue engine from visiting fans. Strategy:

- **"Island Rivalry Package" with BC Ferries:** Partner with BC Ferries to offer a bundled "Game Day Getaway" -- discounted ferry + $10 concession credit loaded onto a digital voucher redeemable at any SOFMC concession point. BC Ferries benefits from incremental weekend/evening ridership on the Tsawwassen-Swartz Bay route. Estimated cost to Royals: $0 (partner-funded). Revenue: incremental concession sales from credit redemption (fans typically spend 1.5-2x the credit value).
- **"Giants Welcome Station":** Set up a visiting fan welcome area near the main entrance with a QR code linking to a digital concession menu, arena map, and a first-visit discount code ($2 off first concession purchase). Cost: <$200 in signage.
- **Rival-Themed Concession Specials:** "Beat the Giants Burger" or "Mainland Meltdown Poutine" -- limited-time menu items available only during Giants games. Creates social media buzz and FOMO. Work with existing concession supplier; no new equipment needed. Cost: menu design and signage, ~$300.
- **Social Media Provocation:** Pre-game social content targeting Giants fan groups and Vancouver hockey communities. "Come watch your Giants lose in Canada's most beautiful arena city." Cost: staff time only.

**Estimated incremental concession revenue per Giants series: $5,000-$15,000**

**Tier 2: US Division Teams (Everett, Seattle)**

Cross-border fans face friction (passport, border wait) but ferry access from Anacortes to Sidney is scenic and relatively easy. Strategy:

- **"Cross-Border Hockey Weekend" Package:** Partner with Washington State Ferries (Anacortes-Sidney) and a downtown Victoria hotel to offer a two-night package: Friday arrival, Saturday game, Sunday brunch/explore, ferry home. The hotel partner funds the marketing; Royals provide group ticket pricing.
- **Concession "Welcome to Canada" Special:** Poutine + Canadian craft beer combo at a slight discount for fans showing a US ID or Washington State Ferries boarding pass. Fun, on-brand, and drives concession trial.
- **Target Everett and Seattle youth hockey families:** These families already travel for tournaments. Position a Royals game as a family weekend add-on.

**Estimated incremental concession revenue per US series: $2,000-$5,000**

**Tier 3: Prairie Teams (Red Deer, Edmonton, Calgary)**

These teams visit only once per season but draw remarkably well (2,896-4,217). The novelty factor is real. Strategy:

- **"Prairie Night" Themed Events:** When Edmonton visits, run an "Alberta Night" with BBQ-themed concession specials (smoked brisket sandwich, Alberta beef sliders). When Calgary visits, partner with a local business to offer "Stampede Sliders." Cost: $500-$1,000 in specialty menu development per event.
- **Advance Marketing Push:** These games should be marketed 6-8 weeks out with targeted digital ads to local fans emphasizing the rarity: "Edmonton visits Victoria ONCE this season. Don't miss it."
- **Premium Pricing Opportunity:** Consider dynamic concession bundle pricing on high-demand nights -- not higher base prices, but more premium combo offerings (e.g., a $24 "Prairie Feast" combo: burger + fries + craft beer).

**Estimated incremental concession revenue per Prairie visit: $3,000-$8,000**

**Tier 4: BC Division (Kamloops, Kelowna, Prince George, Penticton)**

These opponents visit 2-4 times each but rarely bring large traveling fan bases due to distance. Focus is on making these games attractive to LOCAL fans rather than capturing visitors.

- Use BC Division midweek games as the anchor for community group nights (see Section 7)
- Run "BC Battle" concession specials featuring rotating BC craft products

### 2. Local Business Partnership Network

#### 2.1 The "Royals District" Concept

SOFMC sits at 1925 Blanshard Street in downtown Victoria, surrounded by one of the densest concentrations of restaurants, breweries, and retail in Greater Victoria. The strategy is to formalize a "Royals District" -- a network of 15-25 businesses within a 10-minute walk of the arena that cross-promote game nights.

**How it works:**

- Partner businesses display Royals branding and QR-code-linked game schedules
- On game nights, partners offer "Show Your Ticket" discounts (10-15% off pre-game dinner, post-game drinks)
- Royals promote partners on video boards, social media, and in-app notifications
- Partners pay a modest annual partnership fee ($500-$2,000 depending on tier) or provide in-kind product for concession specials
- All promotions drive traffic both ways: to the partner before/after, to the arena for the game

**Cost to Royals: Minimal** (partnership coordinator time, signage design). **Revenue: $7,500-$50,000/year** in partnership fees, plus incremental attendance driving concession sales.

#### 2.2 Craft Beverage Trail

Victoria's craft beverage scene is a competitive advantage no other WHL market can match. Phillips Brewing and Strait & Narrow already have branded bars in SOFMC. Expand this:

- **"Royals Craft Trail" Passport:** A digital passport (QR-based, no app required) where fans earn stamps for visiting partner breweries/distilleries AND attending Royals games. Complete the trail, earn a limited-edition Royals x Brewery collab item (branded glass, toque, etc.). Partners: Phillips, Driftwood, Hoyne, Whistle Buoy, Herald Street Brew Works, Strait & Narrow, Victoria Distillers.
- **"Brewer's Pick" Rotating Tap:** Each month, feature a different local brewery's flagship beer at the Phillips Bar and Island Canteen, with a $1 per pour donation to a community charity chosen by that brewery. Creates media stories, drives trial, and deepens community ties.
- **Cost:** Passport platform (free using Google Forms or a simple web app), co-funded signage ($500 split across 6-8 partners). **Revenue:** Increased craft beer sales at SOFMC (current data shows strong Phillips and craft beer demand at the Phillips Bar), plus $3,000-$6,000 in annual trail partnership fees.

#### 2.3 Restaurant Partnerships

Target restaurants within a 10-minute walk for pre-game and post-game promotions:

- **Pre-Game:** Partner restaurants offer a "Power Play Pre-Game" menu (quick-service options, 5:00-6:30 PM on game nights) with a Royals game ticket QR code check for 10% discount. Drives foot traffic to restaurants AND ensures fans arrive at the arena with a positive experience anchored to the Royals brand.
- **Post-Game:** "Third Period Specials" at partner bars/restaurants from 9:30 PM-close on game nights. Fans show their digital ticket stub for a drink special or appetizer deal.
- **Revenue model:** Restaurants pay $500-$1,500/season for the partnership; Royals provide video board mentions, social media promotion, and inclusion in the Royals app/website game day guide.

#### 2.4 Hotel Partnerships (Visiting Fan Capture)

Partner with 3-5 downtown hotels for a "Royals Getaway" package:

- Hotel provides a discounted rate for game weekends (they benefit from filling rooms on non-peak nights)
- Package includes $10 SOFMC concession credit per guest (funded by hotel as a room amenity)
- Royals provide a "VIP Visiting Fan" digital welcome with arena guide and concession recommendations
- Target: Vancouver Giants weekends, US team weekends, and holiday tournaments
- **Cost to Royals: $0** (hotel-funded). **Revenue:** Incremental concession sales from credit redemption, plus potential hotel partnership fee ($1,000-$3,000/season per hotel).

#### 2.5 Retail and Service Partnerships

- **Peninsula Co-op (existing sponsor):** "Fuel Up for the Game" -- show your Co-op receipt from game day for $2 off a concession combo. Co-op benefits from game-day fuel-ups and store visits.
- **Local fitness/yoga studios:** "Burn It to Earn It" -- attend a partner class on game day, receive a concession discount. Targets the health-conscious Victoria demographic and creates an unlikely-but-memorable brand crossover.
- **UVic Bookstore/Campus Partners:** Student-specific cross-promotions (see Section 6).

### 3. Community Hub Positioning

#### 3.1 Vision: "Our City, Our Club" as Operating Philosophy

The Royals tagline "Our City, Our Club" is the foundation for a community hub strategy. The goal is to make SOFMC the place where Victoria gathers -- not just for hockey, but as a community anchor.

**Principles:**
- Every game night should feel like a community event, not just a sporting event
- The concession experience should celebrate Victoria's food/beverage identity
- Non-game-day activation generates year-round community connection (and potential concession revenue from events)
- Youth hockey integration turns families into lifelong customers

#### 3.2 Youth Hockey Integration

Greater Victoria has a large minor hockey ecosystem. These families represent the highest lifetime value segment:

- **"Future Royals" Program:** Every minor hockey player in Greater Victoria gets a Royals card entitling them to 2 free tickets per season (weekday games) and a $3 concession voucher per visit. Parents buy their own tickets and drive additional concession spend. Cost: ~$2-$5 per card x 2,000 players = $4,000-$10,000 in ticket/concession subsidy. Revenue: 2,000 families x 2 adult tickets x $15 avg ticket = $60,000 in ticket revenue, plus $8-$12 per-cap concession spend per family = $16,000-$24,000 in concession revenue. **Net positive by a wide margin.**
- **Minor Hockey Night:** 4-6 times per season (targeting low-attendance Wednesday games), invite local minor hockey associations for group rate tickets ($5-$8). Teams get on-ice recognition during intermission. Parents fill the concessions. Estimated 200-400 incremental fans per event = $1,600-$4,800 in concession revenue per event.
- **Post-Game Skate:** After select games, open the ice for a "Skate with the Royals" where minor hockey kids can skate alongside WHL players. Builds lifelong fan loyalty.

#### 3.3 Corporate Community Programs

- **"Royals Corporate Challenge":** Companies purchase group ticket packages (10-50 seats) for a discounted rate. Include a concession voucher bundle ($50-$100 in concession credits). The corporate buyer gets team-building; SOFMC gets guaranteed concession revenue.
- **"Community Suite":** Designate a section (not a luxury suite, just a blocked section of 20-30 seats) that rotates each game to a different nonprofit, school group, or community organization at no cost. The organization fills seats that would otherwise be empty, and those attendees buy concessions. Marketing partner sponsors the "Community Suite" for naming rights ($5,000-$10,000/season).
- **Government Employee Nights:** Victoria is a government town. Partner with the BC Public Service to offer group rates on select midweek games. Target specific ministries or departments for friendly competitions ("Ministry of Finance vs. Ministry of Health: Who's Louder?").

#### 3.4 Non-Game-Day Activation

While the primary concession revenue comes from game nights, SOFMC can generate incremental community engagement (and some concession testing) through:

- **Royals Watch Parties:** For WHL playoff games on the road, open SOFMC (or a partner venue) for watch parties with concession service. Cost: staff time + AV. Revenue: bar/food sales on otherwise dark nights.
- **Community Market Days:** Monthly Saturday morning markets in the concourse (off-season) featuring local vendors. Positions SOFMC as a community space and introduces non-hockey fans to the venue. Vendor table fees cover costs.
- **Private Event Rentals:** Market the Phillips Bar and Strait & Narrow areas for corporate events, holiday parties, and fundraisers during non-game-day periods. These spaces are underutilized assets.

### 4. Economic Impact Framework

#### 4.1 Measuring the Royals' Economic Footprint

To tell the economic impact story to city council, sponsors, and media, use a three-tier framework:

**Tier 1: Direct Spending (most credible, easiest to measure)**
- Total annual gate revenue (ticket sales x games)
- Total annual concession revenue (from POS data -- already tracked)
- Total annual merchandise revenue
- Total annual sponsorship/partnership revenue
- Royals payroll and operational spending in Victoria

**Tier 2: Induced Spending (visiting fan economic impact)**
- Visiting fans per season (estimate from attendance data: Vancouver games + US/Prairie games)
- Average spending per visiting fan OUTSIDE the arena (hotel, restaurant, ferry, gas, retail)
- Formula: Visiting Fans x Avg. External Spend = Induced Economic Impact

**Estimated calculation:**
- ~8,000-12,000 visiting fans per season (conservative, based on attendance premiums on rival games)
- Average external spend per visiting fan: $80-$200 (lower for day-trippers, higher for overnighters)
- **Induced impact: $640,000 - $2,400,000 per season**

**Tier 3: Multiplier Effects**
- Standard economic multiplier for sports/entertainment: 1.5-2.0x
- Apply to Tier 1 + Tier 2 for total community impact
- **Estimated total economic impact: $3,000,000 - $8,000,000 per season** (including direct + induced + multiplier)

#### 4.2 Telling the Story

- **To City Council:** "The Victoria Royals generate an estimated $3-8M in annual economic impact for the City of Victoria, supporting over 100 seasonal jobs and driving visitor spending at downtown businesses on 30+ event nights per year."
- **To Sponsors:** "Your partnership reaches not just 2,600 fans per game, but the broader economic ecosystem of downtown Victoria businesses that benefit from game-night traffic."
- **To Media:** Human-interest stories about visiting fan families who make the ferry trip a tradition, local businesses that see 30% revenue lifts on game nights, and youth hockey families whose Saturday routine revolves around SOFMC.

#### 4.3 Data Collection for Impact Measurement

- Track visiting fan origin via optional ticket registration or post-game survey QR codes
- Partner with downtown businesses to measure game-night revenue lifts (anonymized, aggregated)
- Annual "Economic Impact Report" co-produced with a UVic business school capstone project (free labor, academic credibility)

### 5. Tourism Integration

#### 5.1 The Calendar Challenge

Hockey season (September-April) and cruise ship season (May-October) have minimal overlap (September-October only). Conference tourism is more aligned but variable. Strategy must be realistic about the narrow tourism-hockey window.

#### 5.2 BC Ferries Partnership (Highest Priority)

BC Ferries is the gateway for the Royals' largest visiting fan base (Vancouver/Lower Mainland) and the single most impactful tourism partner:

- **"Sail & Score" Package:** BC Ferries co-brands a game-day package on their website and in-terminal screens. Tsawwassen-Swartz Bay round trip + Royals game ticket + $10 concession credit. BC Ferries benefits from incremental evening/weekend ridership (ferries are under-capacity on many Friday/Saturday evening sailings).
- **In-Terminal Promotion:** Royals schedule posters and QR codes in Tsawwassen and Swartz Bay terminals. Cost: shared between partners. "Arriving in Victoria? Catch a Royals game tonight!"
- **Data Sharing:** BC Ferries can provide anonymized ridership data for game-day sailings, helping quantify the visiting fan economic impact.

**Cost to Royals: Near $0** (co-funded). **Revenue: $10,000-$30,000/season** in incremental ticket and concession sales from package uptake.

#### 5.3 Conference & Business Travel Integration

- **Tourism Victoria Partnership:** Include Royals game information in conference bid packages for events held September-April. "Your delegates can experience live WHL hockey during their stay."
- **Hotel Concierge Program:** Provide downtown hotel concierge desks with Royals game schedules, QR-code ticket links, and a "local experience" description. Conference attendees and business travelers looking for evening entertainment become incremental fans and concession customers.
- **Cost:** Printed/digital materials ($500-$1,000/season). **Revenue:** Even 50 conference attendees per season at $12 concession per-cap = $600. Small but zero-effort once set up.

#### 5.4 September/October Tourism Window

The two months where hockey and tourism overlap:

- **"Welcome Back to Hockey" Opening Night Event:** Market heavily to tourists and seasonal visitors as a cultural experience. Partner with Tourism Victoria to include in October event listings.
- **Cruise Ship Day-of Promotion (limited):** For ships docked on game days in September/October, work with shore excursion operators to offer a "Canadian Hockey Experience" option. Realistic volume: 20-50 cruise passengers per game on 2-4 overlap dates. Small but high per-cap (these are high-spending tourists).

### 6. Cultural & Community Event Programming

#### 6.1 First Nations / Indigenous Inclusion

Victoria is on the traditional territory of the Lekwungen peoples (Songhees and Esquimalt Nations). Meaningful inclusion is both the right thing to do and a community engagement opportunity:

- **Indigenous Heritage Night (1-2 per season):** Partner with local First Nations communities for pre-game ceremony, Indigenous art installations in the concourse, and a concession special featuring Indigenous-inspired cuisine (bannock tacos, smoked salmon sliders). Revenue split with Indigenous food vendors.
- **Land Acknowledgment Integration:** Ensure the existing land acknowledgment practice is visible and sincere, not perfunctory. Include it in digital game-day guides.
- **Indigenous Youth Hockey Support:** Sponsor or co-host an Indigenous youth hockey clinic with Royals players. Builds genuine community relationships.

**Cost:** $1,000-$3,000 per event (honoraria, food vendor coordination). **Revenue:** Themed night attendance lift + specialty concession sales. More importantly, positions the Royals as a respectful community partner.

#### 6.2 Military Appreciation (CFB Esquimalt)

Victoria's military community (CFB Esquimalt, Pacific Fleet) is a large, loyal, and under-targeted fan segment:

- **Military Appreciation Night (2-3 per season):** Discounted tickets for military families, on-ice ceremony, and a specialty concession item (proceeds to a military family charity). Partner with CANEX or the Military Family Resource Centre.
- **"Defend the Rink" Section:** Offer a standing block of 50-100 discounted tickets for military personnel on every game night. Even at $8-$10/ticket, these are seats that would otherwise be empty, and the concession revenue ($8-$12 per-cap) more than compensates.
- **CFB Esquimalt Group Bookings:** Market to individual units and ships' companies for team-building events. Navy ships returning to port often have morale budgets for group outings.

**Cost:** Ticket subsidy ($2,000-$5,000/season). **Revenue:** $10,000-$25,000 in incremental concession revenue from military fans who would not otherwise attend.

#### 6.3 University & College Nights

UVic (21,000+ students), Camosun College (18,000+ students), and Royal Roads University represent a huge untapped market of young fans with high social media engagement:

- **"Student Rush" Program:** $10 student tickets (day-of, weekday games) with student ID. Students drive concession sales, especially beer and snack categories.
- **UVic Night (2-3 per season):** Partner with UVic Student Society for bus transportation from campus, group ticket pricing, and a concession discount for UVic ID holders. Have Royals players visit campus for promotion.
- **Intramural Hockey Night:** Invite UVic and Camosun intramural hockey teams for group outings with on-ice intermission activities.
- **"Exam Break Hockey":** January and April exam periods -- market games as stress relief. "$5 Student Stress Buster" tickets on select midweek games.

**Cost:** Ticket subsidy ($1,000-$3,000/season for discounted tickets). **Revenue:** Student fans are high-frequency concession buyers (beer, snacks). 200 incremental students per event x 4 events x $10 per-cap = $8,000 in concession revenue.

#### 6.4 Pride Night

- **Annual Pride Night:** Partner with Victoria Pride Society for a themed game night with rainbow elements, inclusive messaging, and a donation to a local 2SLGBTQ+ organization.
- **Limited-edition Pride Royals merchandise** (portion of proceeds donated).
- Aligns with Victoria's progressive community identity and broadens the fan base.

#### 6.5 Multicultural Community Events

- **Lunar New Year Night (January/February):** Victoria has a significant Chinese-Canadian community. Themed concession specials, cultural performances during intermissions, partnership with local Chinese community associations.
- **Diwali Night (October/November):** Partner with Victoria's South Asian community for a celebration of lights themed game night.
- **Newcomer Welcome Night:** Partner with the Victoria Immigrant and Refugee Centre to host newcomer families for a free or deeply discounted game experience. "Welcome to Canada -- here's hockey!" Builds lifelong fans.

#### 6.6 Local Arts & Music Integration

- **"Sound of the Royals" Local Music Showcase:** Feature a different local band/musician for pre-game and intermission entertainment on select nights. Victoria's music scene is vibrant. Partner with local venues (Lucky Bar, Capital Ballroom) for cross-promotion.
- **Student Art Night:** Feature art from local high school or UVic art students in the concourse. Builds community pride and brings families who wouldn't otherwise attend.

### 7. Weekday Game Activation

#### 7.1 The Problem

Tuesday and Wednesday games regularly draw 1,200-2,200 fans. In a 7,000-seat arena, this means 4,800-5,800 empty seats and proportionally depressed concession revenue. Our data shows that even the Island Canteen (highest-volume concession point at ~4,500 transactions over a recent 4-game sample) sees significantly reduced volume on low-attendance nights.

**The proof-of-concept:** The $1 Dog Night promotion drew 2,245 on a Wednesday -- a 50-80% lift over typical Wednesday attendance. This demonstrates that price-based concession promotions can move the needle on weekday attendance.

#### 7.2 Community Group Night Program

**Structure:** Designate 10-15 midweek games per season as "Community Group Nights" with a specific community partner for each:

| Week | Partner | Estimated Draw |
|---|---|---|
| Tue Oct | Minor Hockey Association | 200-400 families |
| Wed Oct | CFB Esquimalt Unit Night | 100-200 military |
| Tue Nov | UVic Student Society | 200-400 students |
| Wed Nov | Peninsula Co-op Families | 150-300 families |
| Tue Dec | Government Employee Night | 100-300 government workers |
| Wed Jan | Camosun College Night | 100-200 students |
| Tue Jan | Downtown Victoria Business Assoc. | 100-200 professionals |
| Wed Feb | Chinese New Year Community | 100-200 families |
| Tue Feb | Youth Sports Organizations | 200-400 families |
| Wed Mar | Big Brothers Big Sisters | 100-200 mentors/mentees |
| Tue Mar | School District 61 Educators Night | 100-200 teachers |
| Wed Apr | First Responders Night | 100-200 fire/police/paramedic |

**Ticket pricing:** $8-$12 group rate (vs. regular pricing). Even at discounted rates, the incremental concession revenue ($8-$12 per person) far exceeds the ticket discount.

**Estimated impact:** 150-300 incremental fans per Community Group Night x 12 nights x $10 avg concession per-cap = **$18,000-$36,000 in annual incremental concession revenue.**

#### 7.3 Themed Concession Nights

Building on the $1 Dog Night success, create a rotating calendar of weekday concession promotions:

- **Taco Tuesday:** $3 tacos from TacoTacoTaco (already have the infrastructure). Cross-promote with Cinco agave spirits for a combo deal.
- **Wing Wednesday:** Partner with a local wing supplier for a game-night wing special. Wings + craft beer combo at the Phillips Bar.
- **Family Feast Night (select Wednesdays):** $25 family concession combo (2 hot dogs + 2 kids' drinks + 1 popcorn + 1 adult beverage). Targets the family segment that currently finds concessions expensive.
- **$1 Dog Night Expansion:** The proof-of-concept works. Run it 3-4 times per season on the lowest-projected-attendance games. The hot dog is a loss leader; the incremental beer, pop, and snack sales more than compensate.

**Cost:** $500-$1,500 per promotion (food cost subsidy for loss leaders). **Revenue:** 200-500 incremental fans per promotion x $8-$12 per-cap (beyond the promoted item) = $1,600-$6,000 per event.

#### 7.4 "Two-for-Tuesday" and "Midweek Momentum" Ticket Deals

- **Two-for-Tuesday:** Buy one ticket, get one free on all Tuesday games. Reduces the barrier for bringing a friend. The second ticket is a concession revenue opportunity.
- **Wednesday Season Ticket Add-On:** Offer existing season ticket holders a discounted "Wednesday Pack" add-on (all Wednesday home games for $50). Fills the most underperforming game nights.

#### 7.5 Corporate Weeknight Packages

- **"Team Night Out" Package:** 10 tickets + $100 concession credit + reserved section for $200 (vs. $150+ for tickets alone at full price). Target HR departments and office managers via LinkedIn, Chamber of Commerce, and Downtown Victoria Business Association.
- **Quick-Turnaround Booking:** Make group bookings available with 48-hour lead time. Corporate decisions for team outings are often last-minute.

### 8. Digital Community Building

#### 8.1 "Royals Insider" Digital Platform

Build a lightweight digital engagement layer (not a full app -- use mobile web or an existing platform like Instagram/TikTok) that deepens fan connection and drives concession behavior:

- **Pre-Game Push Notifications:** "Tonight's game vs. Vancouver starts at 7:05. Beat the rush -- concessions open at 6:00. Tonight's special: Mainland Meltdown Poutine at Island Canteen."
- **Digital Loyalty Program:** Points for attendance (check-in via QR at gate) and concession purchases. Rewards: free concession items, merchandise, meet-and-greet with players. Drives repeat attendance and concession spend. Can be built on a simple platform like Square Loyalty (already cashless venue) for minimal cost.
- **Post-Game Content:** Highlight clips, player interviews, and fan cam content shared within 30 minutes of game end. Keeps the Royals top-of-mind between games.

**Cost:** $1,000-$5,000 for platform setup (if using existing tools like Square Loyalty, much less). **Revenue:** Loyalty programs typically increase per-visit spending by 10-20%. On a $10 per-cap across 2,600 avg fans x 30 games, even a 10% lift = $78,000 in annual concession revenue.

#### 8.2 Social Media Community Strategy

- **Fan-Generated Content:** "Best Royals Game Photo" contest with a concession gift card prize. Creates organic reach.
- **"Royals Families" Facebook Group:** A dedicated group for season ticket holders and frequent fans. Post exclusive concession previews, early access to promotions, and community discussions.
- **TikTok / Instagram Reels:** Behind-the-scenes content (players in the community, concession prep, ice making) that builds emotional connection. Target UVic and Camosun students.
- **Opponent-Specific Content:** Before Vancouver games, create rivalry content that gets shared across both fan bases, expanding reach.

#### 8.3 Data-Driven Personalization

Leverage the cashless venue infrastructure:

- **Transaction Data Analysis:** SOFMC already captures every transaction with timestamp, item, and location. Use this to understand purchasing patterns and target promotions (e.g., fans who buy beer but not food get a "Add fries for $2" offer on their next visit).
- **Attendance-Linked Concession Offers:** Fans who attend 5+ games per season receive escalating concession rewards (5th game: free popcorn; 10th game: free beer; 15th game: free combo meal).
- **Predictive Stocking:** Use transaction data to forecast concession demand by opponent, day of week, and promotion type, reducing waste and stockouts.
