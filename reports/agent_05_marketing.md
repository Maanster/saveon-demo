# Agent 5: Marketing Strategy Report
## Victoria Royals -- Concession Marketing & Fan Engagement Plan

---

### Executive Summary

This marketing strategy is designed to increase concession revenue at Save-On-Foods Memorial Centre (SOFMC) by addressing the Victoria Royals' four core challenges: long intermission queues that cost sales, weak weekday attendance (1,200-2,200 vs. 5,500 on peak nights), low average basket size, and insufficient repeat concession visits per game. The plan leverages the Royals' existing strengths -- a cashless arena, six distinct concession stands with unique personalities, strong local beverage partnerships (Phillips Brewing, Strait & Narrow, Cinco), the Eventium mobile ordering platform, and a loyal but undersized fanbase of approximately 2,600 average attendance in a 7,000-seat venue.

Every recommendation is evaluated against WHL budget realities. The strategy prioritizes low-cost/no-cost tactics (social media, operational changes, PA announcements, staff training, partnership co-funding) over expensive infrastructure investments. The total incremental marketing investment recommended is $12,500-$22,000 for the first season, with projected revenue uplift of $75,000-$150,000 annually based on conservative estimates of a 10-15% increase in per-capita concession spend and a 5-10% improvement in weekday attendance.

**Key Revenue Levers:**
- Increasing per-capita spend from an estimated $8-10 to $12-14 through combo engineering and upselling
- Capturing 200-400 lost intermission sales per high-attendance game through queue management and mobile ordering
- Growing weekday attendance by 300-500 fans through themed promotions
- Driving second and third concession visits per game through intermission flash deals
- Activating partner co-investment to offset marketing costs

- **$3 Popcorn + Pop Friday**: Early-bird combo for fans arriving before puck drop

### 2. Upselling & Basket Growth Strategy

#### Current Basket Analysis (from Transaction Data)

The transaction data reveals several patterns that inform upselling strategy:
- **Beer is the dominant category**: Cans of Beer, Draft Beer, and Cider & Coolers appear in a high proportion of transactions
- **Standalone snack purchases are common**: Many transactions show a single pretzel, single popcorn, or single candy -- these are missed upsell opportunities
- **COMBOS exist but appear underutilized**: The data shows "Dog & Chips," "Popcorn & 2 Dogs," "Double Burger & Fries," and "Tenders & Fries" combos, but individual item purchases far outnumber combo purchases
- **Churros are a standout impulse item**: High volume across Island Canteen and TacoTacoTaco, frequently purchased in multiples (2-3 at a time)
- **Tequila slushies (Mangonada) at TacoTacoTaco are a premium opportunity**: $9.99 price point with strong Instagram appeal

#### Combo Engineering: The "Power Play" Combo System

Design a tiered combo system with hockey-themed names:

| Combo Name | Contents | Estimated Price | Savings vs. Individual | Target |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **The Starter** | Hot Dog + Chips + Pop | $12.99 | ~$2.00 | Families, kids |
| **The Power Play** | 2 Dogs + Popcorn + 2 Pops | $19.99 | ~$4.00 | Families of 2-3 |
| **The Hat Trick** | Chicken Tenders + Fries + Beer | $18.99 | ~$2.50 | Adults 19+ |
| **The Enforcer** | Double Burger + Fries + 24oz Draft | $22.99 | ~$3.00 | Heavy spenders |
| **The Fiesta** | 3 Tacos + Tequila Slushy | $18.99 | ~$2.50 | Young adults at TacoTacoTaco |
| **The Blue Line** | 2 Pizza Slices + Pop | $13.99 | ~$1.50 | Quick service at Island Slice |
| **The Overtime** | Pretzel + Churro + Pop | $11.99 | ~$2.00 | Snackers, second visit |
| **The Rookie** (Kids) | Mini Dog + Pop + Gummies | $8.99 | ~$2.00 | Children under 12 |

#### Staff Upselling Protocol

Train concession staff with a simple "1-2-3" upselling framework that takes under 5 seconds per transaction:

1. **Suggest the Combo**: "Would you like to make that a Power Play Combo for just $2 more?"
2. **Add-On Prompt**: "Add a churro for $2?" or "Upgrade to a 24oz for $2?"
3. **Beverage Prompt on Food-Only Orders**: "Can I get you a drink with that?"

**Implementation (Zero Cost):**
- Print laminated upsell prompt cards at each POS station
- 15-minute staff briefing before each game (integrate into existing pre-game huddle)
- Gamify upselling: track which cashier sells the most combos per game, post leaderboard in break room, small prize (free meal voucher) for weekly winner
- Script the Eventium mobile ordering platform to auto-suggest add-ons (churro, upgrade size, add a drink) during the order flow

#### POS-Level Upsell Prompts

Work with the POS provider to implement:
- Automatic "Would you like to add...?" prompt when a food item is rung through without a beverage
- Combo suggestion when individual items that make up a combo are selected
- "Upgrade to 24oz?" prompt on all 12oz draft beer orders
- Highlighted "FEATURED COMBO" button on POS home screen

#### Size Strategy

- Introduce a "game-day size" for popcorn and draft beer (24oz) as the default call-out on menu boards
- Train staff to offer the larger size first: "Large popcorn?" rather than "What size?"
- Price the upgrade from 12oz to 24oz draft at an attractive $3 increment (perceived value: almost double the beer for a modest upcharge)

### 3. Fan Engagement Tactics

#### In-Arena Engagement

1. **"Concession Cam"**: During stoppages, display a live camera feed on the jumbotron of fans enjoying concession items. Feature fans with the most impressive food hauls. Zero cost, drives FOMO and social sharing.

2. **"Best Bite of the Night" Fan Vote**: Use the arena Wi-Fi captive portal or Eventium app to run a nightly poll: "What's the best bite tonight?" between 3-4 featured items. Winning item gets featured next game. Builds engagement and drives concession awareness.

3. **"Predict the Period" Contest**: Fans text or scan a QR code to predict the score at the end of each period. Correct predictions win a concession voucher ($5-$10). Cost: ~$50-100 per game in vouchers; drives return visits to concession stands.

4. **PA Announcements (Scripted, Timed)**: Partner with the PA announcer to deliver 3-4 concession calls per game:
   - Pre-game: "Beat the rush -- head to Island Canteen now for tonight's Power Play Combo!"
   - 1st intermission: "Phillips Bar has Glitterbomb on special this period only -- get it before it's gone!"
   - 2nd intermission: "TacoTacoTaco flash deal: $2 churros for the next 10 minutes!"
   - 3rd period: "Last call reminder: concessions close 5 minutes after the final buzzer."

5. **Seat-Drop Coupons**: On targeted weeknight games, place a simple printed coupon on every seat: "$2 off any combo tonight." Cost: ~$30 in printing per game for 2,500 sheets. Drives first concession visit.

6. **"Puck Drop Deal"**: A mystery concession deal revealed only at puck drop. Announced on social media pre-game as "Tonight's Puck Drop Deal will be revealed at 7:05!" Drives early arrival AND social media engagement.

#### Interactive Experiences

7. **"Build the Menu" Fan Voting (Monthly)**: Let fans vote on one rotating menu item each month via Instagram Stories poll or the Royals' website. Options like "Loaded Poutine vs. Jalapeño Poppers" or "Mango Slushy vs. Strawberry Slushy." Winning item becomes the "Fan's Choice" for the month. Zero cost; massive engagement.

8. **"Sauce Boss" Challenge at TacoTacoTaco**: Fans who order the spiciest hot sauce option and finish their tacos get their photo on the "Wall of Flame" (a poster board near TacoTacoTaco). Share on social media with #RoyalsSauceBoss. Zero cost.

9. **Kid's Zone Concession Activity**: Coloring sheets with concession item designs (churro character, hot dog mascot) given to families. Completed sheets earn a free small popcorn. Builds family loyalty, costs ~$0.10/sheet.

10. **Reusable Cup Promotion**: Expand the Friendlier reusable cup program with a Royals-branded cup. First fill at full price, every refill saves $1. Drives repeat beverage purchases and aligns with Victoria's eco-conscious culture. Partner with Friendlier on co-branding.

### 5. Loyalty & Rewards Program Design

#### Program Name: "Royal Rewards"

A simple, digital-first loyalty program designed for WHL budget constraints. No app development required -- use an existing platform like Square Loyalty, Stamp Me, or a simple QR-code-based system integrated with the existing cashless POS.

#### Structure: Points-Based with Milestone Rewards

| Action | Points Earned |
|---|---|
| Any concession purchase | 1 point per $1 spent |
| Purchase a combo | Bonus 5 points |
| Purchase on a weeknight game (Tue/Wed) | 2x points |
| Refer a friend who buys a ticket | 50 bonus points |
| Social media post with #RoyalsEats | 10 points (verified by staff via QR scan) |

#### Reward Tiers

| Points | Reward |
|---|---|
| 25 points | Free regular popcorn |
| 50 points | Free churro or pretzel |
| 100 points | Free combo (any "Starter" tier) |
| 200 points | Free 24oz Phillips draft beer |
| 500 points | "VIP Game Night" -- 2 tickets + $30 concession credit + ReMax Fan Deck access |

#### Digital Punch Card Alternative (Lowest Cost)

If a full points system is too complex to launch in Season 1, implement a digital punch card via Stamp Me (free tier available) or a simple printed card:

- **"5th Beer Free"**: Buy 4 beers (any type), get the 5th free. Drives repeat beverage visits.
- **"Combo Collector"**: Buy 5 combos across the season, get a free combo. Encourages combo adoption.
- **"TacoTacoTaco Regular"**: Buy 3 taco orders, get a free churro. Builds TacoTacoTaco loyalty.

#### Season Ticket Holder Perks (Zero Incremental Cost)

- **"First Pour" Early Entry**: Season ticket holders get early arena entry (15 minutes before general doors) with exclusive access to concession stands. Reduces peak queues AND rewards loyalty.
- **Birthday Recognition**: Automatically send a birthday concession voucher ($10) to season ticket holders. Estimated cost: $500-750/season based on ~50-75 STH birthdays.
- **"10th Game Bonus"**: After attending 10 games, season ticket holders receive a free Royals-branded reusable cup.

### 7. Weekday Game Revenue Growth

Weekday games (Tuesday and Wednesday) are the Royals' biggest revenue gap. Average attendance of 1,200-2,200 vs. 3,500-5,500 on Fridays/Saturdays represents 2,000-3,000 empty seats per game. Each empty seat represents an estimated $8-14 in lost concession revenue.

#### The Weekday Attendance Formula

The $1 Dog Night experiment proved the concept: a compelling, value-driven reason can nearly double Wednesday attendance (2,245 vs. ~1,500 typical). The strategy is to replicate this model with variety.

**Rotating Weekday Promotions:**

| Week | Tuesday | Wednesday |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Taco Tuesday ($2 off tacos) | $1 Dog Night |
| 2 | Taco Tuesday ($2 off tacos) | $3 Popcorn & Pop Night |
| 3 | Taco Tuesday ($2 off tacos) | Wing Wednesday (Tenders Deal) |
| 4 | Taco Tuesday ($2 off tacos) | $1 Dog Night |

- **Taco Tuesday is fixed** (builds brand recognition for TacoTacoTaco)
- **Wednesday rotates** to prevent fatigue while maintaining the value proposition

#### Corporate / Group Packages with Concession Credits

Target Victoria's large employer base (BC Government, University of Victoria, Island Health, CFB Esquimalt):

1. **"Team Night Out" Package**: 10+ tickets at group rate + $5 concession credit per person + reserved seating block. Priced at $25-30/person all-in.
2. **"Company Challenge"**: Two companies buy group packages for the same game. A fun in-arena competition (trivia, relay race during intermission) with bragging rights and a trophy.
3. **Target**: Book 5-10 corporate groups per month for weeknight games = 50-100 additional fans per game minimum, each with guaranteed concession spend.

#### Student Night (Tuesday or Wednesday)

- **$10 student tickets** (with valid UVic/Camosun/Royal Roads ID)
- **$5 "Student Meal Deal"**: Hot dog + pop (loss leader to get students through the door)
- **Promote through**: Student union social media, campus posters, residence hall advisors, first-year orientation packages
- **"Student Section" Atmosphere**: Designate a section, play student-friendly music during warm-ups, feature student groups on jumbotron
- **Estimated impact**: 200-500 additional students per game at low acquisition cost

#### Military Appreciation (Monthly -- Targeting CFB Esquimalt)

- **Free or discounted tickets** for active military (coordinate with MFRCs -- Military Family Resource Centres)
- **"Salute Combo"** at 15% discount for military ID holders
- **National anthem participation** by CFB Esquimalt personnel
- **Promote through**: Esquimalt base bulletin boards, MFRC newsletters, military family Facebook groups
- **Cost**: Comp ticket value offset by concession revenue from military families (typically 3-4 people per family unit)

#### Happy Hour Concept (Pre-Game, Weeknights Only)

- **"Early Bird Happy Hour"**: 5:30-7:05 PM on Tuesday/Wednesday games

### 8. Intermission Marketing (Driving Sales During Breaks)

The 18-minute intermission is the highest-revenue window and the biggest operational bottleneck. Current pain point: fans abandon queues and return to seats without purchasing, representing an estimated $2,000-5,000 in lost revenue per high-attendance game.

#### Queue Management Strategies

1. **Stagger Flash Deals Across Stands**
   - 1st Intermission: Flash deal at Phillips Bar (beer special)
   - 2nd Intermission: Flash deal at TacoTacoTaco (churro deal)
   - This distributes foot traffic across stands rather than concentrating it at Island Canteen
   - PA announcements direct traffic: "This intermission's flash deal is at TacoTacoTaco -- $2 churros!"

2. **"Express Lane" for Combos and Pre-Orders**
   - Dedicate one POS station at Island Canteen as a "Combo Express" lane during intermissions
   - Only serves pre-set combos (no customization) = faster throughput
   - Promote Eventium mobile pre-orders: "Skip the line! Order on your phone, pick up at the Express window"

3. **Roaming Vendors in the Stands**
   - Deploy 2-3 roaming vendors during intermissions carrying high-margin, grab-and-go items: canned beer, bottled water, chips, gummies, candy
   - These vendors capture sales from fans who refuse to leave their seats
   - Staff cost: existing part-time staff redeployed; no incremental labour cost if scheduled during shift
   - Revenue capture: estimated 50-100 additional transactions per game at $5-10 average

4. **"Beat the Clock" Intermission Countdown**
   - Display a countdown timer on the jumbotron during intermissions
   - At specific time marks, announce deals:
     - 15:00 remaining: "Intermission flash deal NOW at Phillips Bar!"
     - 10:00 remaining: "Last 10 minutes for the deal!"
     - 5:00 remaining: "5 minutes left -- final call!"
   - Creates urgency, drives faster purchasing decisions

#### Second and Third Visit Incentives

5. **"Come Back for Dessert" Receipt Coupon**
   - Print a coupon on every 1st-period concession receipt: "$1 off any sweet (churro, candy, gummies) in the 2nd period"
   - Drives a second concession visit
   - Cost: minimal (receipt paper + margin on discount); uplift from incremental purchases

6. **"3rd Period Pick-Me-Up"**
   - Discount on coffee, hot chocolate, and soft drinks during the 3rd period
   - Targets the post-intermission lull when concession traffic drops significantly
   - PA announcement: "Third period pick-me-up! $1 off all hot drinks at Van Houtte and the Island Canteen!"

7. **"Power Play = Power Deal"**
   - When the Royals go on a power play, a real-time deal is activated: e.g., "$1 off all beer during the power play!"
   - Announced on jumbotron and PA; creates excitement tied to game action
   - Frequency: estimated 3-5 power plays per game, each lasting ~2 minutes
   - Short duration limits margin impact; excitement drives impulse purchases

#### Mobile Ordering Optimization

8. **Eventium Push Notifications**
   - If Eventium supports push notifications, send timed alerts:
     - End of 1st period: "Beat the line! Pre-order your 1st intermission snack now."
     - End of 2nd period: "Craving churros? Order now for instant pickup at TacoTacoTaco."
   - If push notifications aren't supported, use the arena Wi-Fi captive portal to display a "Pre-order now" banner when fans reconnect at intermission.

9. **Pre-Order Incentive**
   - Offer a small discount ($0.50-$1.00) or bonus loyalty points for pre-ordering via Eventium
   - Reduces queue length AND increases conversion (committed orders don't abandon)
   - Display estimated pickup time to set expectations

### 10. Marketing Recommendations (Numbered List)

1. **Launch the "Power Play Combo" system** across all concession stands with hockey-themed names and tiered pricing. This is the single highest-impact revenue driver -- moving even 20% of individual-item purchasers to combos could increase average transaction value by 25-35%.

2. **Continue and expand $1 Dog Night** to a bi-weekly Wednesday occurrence. It is the only proven promotion in the current data that nearly doubled midweek attendance. Replicate the formula with "$2 Churro Night" and "$3 Popcorn & Pop Night" on alternating weeks.

3. **Implement Taco Tuesday as a permanent weekly fixture** at TacoTacoTaco with $2 off tacos and $1 off tequila slushies. This creates a recurring reason for young adults and students to attend Tuesday games, directly addressing the weakest attendance day.

4. **Train all concession staff on the "1-2-3 Upsell"** protocol with laminated prompt cards at every POS station. This zero-cost initiative should be implemented before the next home game. Target: increase combo attachment rate to 30%+ of transactions within 60 days.

5. **Deploy 2-3 roaming concession vendors** during intermissions carrying beer, water, chips, and candy to capture sales from fans who will not join queues. Reuse existing part-time staff during their shifts.

6. **Activate Eventium mobile pre-ordering aggressively** with a $0.50 discount or bonus loyalty points for pre-orders. Promote with PA announcements, signage at every stand ("Skip the line -- order on your phone!"), and QR codes on seat backs or armrests.

7. **Launch the "Royal Rewards" loyalty program** using a free or low-cost digital platform (Stamp Me or Square Loyalty). Start with the digital punch card model in Season 1, graduate to full points-based system in Season 2 once behaviour data is collected.

8. **Execute the Phillips Brewing "Brewery to Boards" package** as a joint product sold on both channels. This is a zero-cost, high-impact new revenue stream that introduces Phillips' customer base to the Royals and vice versa.

9. **Implement staggered intermission flash deals** across different stands to distribute foot traffic. Announce via PA and jumbotron: "This intermission's deal is at Phillips Bar!" next intermission at TacoTacoTaco. Reduces Island Canteen bottleneck.

10. **Create a "Student Night" program** in partnership with UVic, Camosun, and Royal Roads student unions. $10 tickets + $5 Student Meal Deal on Tuesday or Wednesday games. Student unions promote to 30,000+ combined students at zero cost to the Royals.

11. **Build a concession-focused social media content strategy** centred on Instagram and TikTok, featuring the tequila slushies, churros, Phillips craft beer, and loaded combos as visual anchors. Assign a student intern or existing marketing staff to capture 2-3 short videos per game.

12. **Engage 10-15 local Victoria micro-influencers** across the season with complimentary tickets and $50-75 concession credits in exchange for 1 Instagram post + 2 Stories per visit. Total cost: ~$1,500-2,000/season for 50,000-225,000 impressions.

13. **Implement "Come Back for Dessert" receipt coupons** on 1st-period purchases, offering $1 off any sweet item in the 2nd period. Drives second concession visits at minimal cost (receipt printing + small margin reduction).

14. **Launch "Power Play = Power Deal"** promotions tied to in-game action. When the Royals go on a power play, $1 off beer is activated. Creates excitement, ties concession spending to on-ice action, and the 2-minute duration limits margin impact.

15. **Negotiate a Peninsula Co-op "Community Night"** where Co-op funds $5 concession vouchers for ticket buyers on one weeknight game per month. Zero cost to Royals; drives weekday attendance through Co-op's member base.

16. **Develop a "Dine & Dash to the Game" partnership** with 2-3 downtown Victoria restaurants for reciprocal discounts (show restaurant receipt for $2 off a combo at SOFMC, show Royals ticket stub for a restaurant deal). Zero cost, builds a game-day dining ecosystem.

17. **Introduce "Early Bird Happy Hour"** on weeknight games (5:30-7:05 PM) with $2 off draft and $1 off cans. Drives early arrival, reduces peak queuing, and creates a social pre-game atmosphere for the young adult demographic.

18. **Install a "Concession Cam" segment** during game stoppages, featuring fans enjoying concession items on the jumbotron. Zero cost, drives FOMO, and generates social media sharing when fans see themselves on screen.

19. **Run a monthly "#RoyalsEats Photo Contest"** where the best fan food photo wins a combo voucher pack. Creates a steady stream of free user-generated content for social media and builds community.

20. **Offer season ticket holders "First Pour" early entry** (15 minutes before general doors) with exclusive concession access. Rewards loyalty, reduces peak queue pressure, and increases the likelihood of multiple concession visits.
